About Us

AgriVision Tunisia is a non-profit organization committed to promoting sustainable agriculture and protecting Tunisia's natural resources. We work with farmers, youth, and local communities to restore degraded lands, encourage eco-friendly farming practices, and raise awareness about the importance of preserving biodiversity. Our goal is to build a future where agriculture and nature grow together productively, responsibly, and sustainably.

Our Mission

We strive to combat land degradation and resource overuse by promoting sustainable farming, biodiversity conservation, and community empowerment across Tunisia.

December 2, 2026

AgriVision Innovation Challenge Launched

We're excited to announce the launch of the "AgriVision Innovation Challenge" for eco-project pitches, offering mentorship, workshops, and seed funding with local investors.

This groundbreaking initiative will support 3 winning projects with comprehensive financial and technical assistance. Participants will benefit from expert mentorship, specialized workshops, and direct access to local investors committed to sustainable agriculture. The challenge aims to foster innovation in eco-friendly farming solutions and empower the next generation of agricultural entrepreneurs in Tunisia.

We're proud to announce that we'll be hosting the first AgriVision Agro Expo showcasing sustainable farming tools, eco-projects, and local produce, inviting investors, NGOs, universities, and farmers.

This landmark event will feature workshops, exhibitions, and networking spaces with expected outcomes including 1000+ visitors engaged in sustainable agriculture awareness, 20+ exhibitors presenting innovative tools and projects, and 10+ partnerships established with NGOs, investors, and academic institutions. The expo will significantly increase visibility for AgriVision's mission on SDG 15 and strengthen collaboration between local farmers, startups, and global associations.

March 8, 2026

Community Training Program Success

Over 500 farmers completed our organic farming certification program, marking a significant milestone in sustainable agriculture education.

The comprehensive 6-month program covered soil health, natural pest control, and organic certification processes. Graduates reported an average 25% increase in crop value and improved soil quality on their farms.

February 28, 2026

Reforestation Initiative Plants 10,000 Trees

Our volunteers and community partners successfully planted 10,000 native trees across three provinces, contributing to Tunisia's green recovery.

The tree-planting campaign involved 300 volunteers and focused on native species that support local ecosystems. These trees will help combat desertification and provide habitat for wildlife while improving air quality.
